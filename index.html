05 / Investment Thesis

Three Independent Pillars

Each pillar is independently sufficient to justify the long position. Together, they compound into an asymmetric risk/reward profile that is rare in public markets.

I

Space Systems is a Defense Infrastructure Business

Not a rocket company

Market Mispricing

Space Systems now represents ~67% of revenue and is anchored by the $816M SDA Tranche III contract — a multi-year, milestone-structured defense relationship. The Space Development Agency's Tracking Layer constellation creates a visible $532M peak revenue recognition event in FY2027, driving EBITDA expansion independent of launch cadence or Neutron execution.

The market prices RKLB at ~45× NTM revenue using small-launch company comps. Defense electronics peers (LHX, NOC) trade at 2–4× revenue — the gap is the mispricing.

$532M FY2027 SDA Peak Revenue
67% Space Systems Revenue Share
$1.85B Total Backlog (+73% YoY)

Revenue Mix Shift (FY2021–2025)

II

Neutron is Upside Optionality, Not the Foundation

A free call option on a 10× TAM expansion

Free Option

The base-case profitability trajectory does not depend on Neutron. However, Neutron expands Rocket Lab's total addressable market by roughly 10×, shifting from the ~$10B small-launch market into the ~$100B+ medium-lift segment.

Even a 5% share of the medium-lift market represents revenue potential exceeding the current company scale. The market currently assigns minimal value to Neutron optionality — making it structurally similar to a deeply out-of-the-money call option that comes at no incremental cost to the investor.

$10B Small-Lift TAM (Current)
$100B+ Medium-Lift TAM (Neutron)
Q4 2026 Maiden Flight Target

TAM Expansion Potential ($B)

III

SpaceX IPO Creates a Sector-Wide Re-Rating

Rocket Lab is the only liquid public proxy

Macro Catalyst

SpaceX's confidential S-1 (filed early April 2026), targeting a mid-2026 listing at an estimated $1.75T valuation, is the largest potential IPO in history. This event forces institutional capital to build dedicated space infrastructure mandates — and Rocket Lab is the only liquid, vertically integrated public proxy.

Short interest has declined approximately 65% from its peak, indicating institutional repositioning is already underway ahead of sector-level capital inflows. The positioning shift precedes the re-rating trigger.
